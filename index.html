Welcome to The Educator’s Guide to the Galaxy

Teaching was already a rollercoaster. Then we added AI, and suddenly the loop-de-loops came with data privacy dilemmas, bias detection puzzles, and robots that promise to grade essays but can’t find their own commas.

This is your friendly, slightly irreverent guide to understanding, teaching with, and surviving AI in education — ethically.

Because if AI is going to join your classroom, it might as well play by the rules.

  • How to spot ethical vs. sketchy AI tools
  • Lesson plans for teaching AI literacy to students
  • Mildly terrifying case studies (and how to avoid them)

For School Leaders

  • Creating school-wide AI policies (that make sense)
  • Checklists for reviewing AI tools before they enter your classrooms
  • How to talk to parents about AI (without causing panic)

Why AI Ethics Matters

AI can personalize learning, automate grading, and free up your time — but it can also reinforce bias, invade privacy, and leave teachers holding the bag when things go wrong.

Ethical AI isn’t just about compliance — it’s about trusting the tools you use and protecting your students.
